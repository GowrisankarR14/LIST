    2. History of Cricket...

    Initially a children's game, it gradually evolved into a sport for adults by the 17th century. The game spread globally through the British Empire, becoming particularly popular in South Asia, Australia, New Zealand, the United Kingdom, Southern Africa, and theWest Indies .

    1. Background

      2. England, likely in the 16th century, as a children's game. It evolved from simpler bat-and-ball games and gained popularity among adults in the 17th century, becoming a professional sport by the 18th century. The first recorded match was in Kent, England, and the game's laws were first created in 1744. International cricket began in the mid-19th century, with the first Test matches between England and Australia.

    2. Foundation

      3. Cricket's history begins in the late 16th century in England, evolving from earlier bat-and-ball games. The first definite written reference dates to the end of the 1500s

    3. Expansions and termination

      4. Cricket's history includes periods of both expansion and contraction, particularly in its international and domestic formats. The sport spread globally through British colonialism, with early international matches and the formation of the ICC (originally the Imperial Cricket Conference) marking significant expansion. However, issues like apartheid in South Africa and financial disputes led to temporary or permanent terminations of teams and leagues

  2. Administration


    3. Administration of Cricket...

    Cricket administration involves managing the operations, governance, and development of the sport at various levels, from local clubs to international bodies. It includes organizing matches, handling logistics, player management, and ensuring adherence to rules and regulations.

  3. Organisation




    4. Organisation...

    A cricket organization oversees the promotion, regulation, and development of cricket within a specific region or globally. It manages competitions, enforces rules, supports player development, and works to grow the sport's popularity.

    1. Tournament format

      2. A cricket tournament format defines how teams compete, such as round-robin, knockout, or league-based systems. The format impacts scheduling, progression, and the overall structure of the competition.

    2. Player acquisition,squad composition and salaries

      3. Cricket player acquisition involves selecting players through auctions, drafts, or transfers based on team needs and performance. Squad composition and salaries are managed within set rules and budgets, balancing star power, roles, and team strategy.

    3. Match rules

      4. Cricket match rules are defined by the **Laws of Cricket**, maintained by the MCC, and govern how the game is played across all formats. Key elements include the number of players, overs per side, methods of dismissal, and fielding restrictions. While the core laws remain constant, formats like Test,ODI, and T20 have specific playing conditions to suit their duration and style.

    4. Prize money

      5. Cricket prize money is awarded to teams and players based on their performance in tournaments and series. Major events like the ICC World Cup or IPL offer substantial prize pools, with winners, runners-up, and standout performers receiving financial rewards. Prize money also serves as an incentive to promote competitiveness and attract talent to the sport.

  4. Teams


    5. Teams...

    Cricket teams represent countries, states, clubs, or franchises and consist of 11 players each, including batters, bowlers, and all-rounders. International teams are governed by national boards like BCCI (India) or ECB (England), while leagues like the IPL or BBL feature franchise teams. Team composition balances player skills, form, and strategies tailored to different match formats.

  7. Teams performance...

    Cricket team performance varies across formats and tournaments. In ODIs, Australia is the most successful with 6 World Cup titles, followed by India and West Indies with 2 each.
